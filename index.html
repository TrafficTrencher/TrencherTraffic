TRAFFIC TRENCHER

Traffic Trencher documents the transition from my medical delivery job as an independent courier to autonomous medical delivery fleet operations.

Today, I operate a real pharmacy delivery route in Southwest Florida as an independent contractor, where income remains directly tied to human time, attention, and physical presence behind the wheel.

The route is streamed daily using Starlink, broadcasting real economic activity directly from the road while human driving is still required.

Self-driving technology is now moving from testing into real-world deployment, with autonomous and Robotaxi-class systems expected to begin entering public use this year.

Rather than waiting for automation to replace my job, Traffic Trencher aligns operationally with that transition as it unfolds.

Miles driven become documented proof of work.

Streaming turns labor into visibility.

This project began while human drivers are still required — not after autonomy arrives.

As autonomous driving moves from testing into real-world deployment, the work being streamed becomes increasingly relevant to the transition already underway.

Visibility becomes capital for automation.

Revenue generated through streaming and creator participation is reinvested toward a single objective:

transitioning from one human-operated medical delivery route into an autonomous medical delivery fleet serving pharmacy partners.

Operational Path

Independent Medical Courier
→ Autonomous Vehicle Deployment
→ Multi-Route Operations
→ Autonomous Medical Delivery Fleet

This is not simulation or speculation. It is a working logistics operation evolving alongside autonomy itself.

Replace my own medical delivery job with autonomy before automation replaces it — then scale into fleet ownership.

Traffic Trencher exists at the inflection point where humans still have to drive — but soon won’t need to.

This transition is being documented live.
